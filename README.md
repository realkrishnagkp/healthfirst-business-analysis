HealthFirst Care — Enhancing Operational Efficiency
Business Analysis capstone project completed as part of the IBM Business Analyst Professional Certificate (Coursera). The project applies end-to-end business analysis to a multispecialty hospital case study: identifying operational failures from raw data, defining requirements, modeling processes, managing scope and risk, and presenting data-driven recommendations.
Business Problem
HealthFirst Care, a six-department hospital (Cardiology, Orthopedics, Neurology, Oncology, Pediatrics, General Medicine), faced:
73% appointment non-completion rate (only 58 of 216 appointments completed)
41.4-minute average patient wait time against a 33-minute target
43% resource unavailability, with only 57% of resources operational at any time
Patient satisfaction of 6.31/10
Root cause: manual phone-based scheduling with no conflict detection, fragmented data silos, and no inter-departmental communication system.
Recommended Solution
Automated scheduling with real-time conflict detection and SMS/email reminders
Real-time resource availability dashboard with maintenance auto-reassignment
Unified Hospital Information System integrating scheduling, records, and billing
Self-service check-in kiosks cutting check-in from 20+ minutes to under 5
Phased 12-month rollout with network stabilization and legacy security patching as go-live prerequisites
Projected outcomes: completion rate 27% to 60%+, wait time down 20%, resource availability above 80%, satisfaction 8.0/10.

Analysis Workflow
Analyzed 646 records across three datasets to quantify operational failures
Documented requirements in a BRD with 10 functional and 6 non-functional requirements
Built an RTM tracing every requirement to a business objective, stakeholder, and test case
Mapped stakeholders on a Power-Interest grid and defined engagement strategies per quadrant
Defined project scope, WBS, and a Change Control Board process
Modeled As-Is workflows, identified bottlenecks, and designed optimized To-Be workflows
Assessed 10 risks by likelihood and impact, with mitigation and contingency plans for the 5 high-severity risks
Consolidated findings into a final presentation with visualizations and recommendations
